### The Microsoft Signal: What we Observed

|                                   | Microsoft's Objectives | Impact on Nonprofits |
|-----------------------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| Al & Cloud Consolidation          | Heavy investment in Copilot, Power Platform, Fabric, and Azure Al - consolidating around the Microsoft Cloud as the primary delivery surface.                        | Larger organizations with genuine Microsoft dependency have a credible, supported path forward. Deep Microsoft investment is a real asset. |
| Dynamics 365 Repositioned         | Dynamics 365 is increasingly one component in a broader Microsoft Cloud strategy - not a standalone CRM offering.                                                      | Small-to-Mid nonprofits find less dedicated investment in CRM-specific nonprofit capability — the roadmap is shaped by commercial B2B priorities. |
| Revenue Model Shifting            | Licensing economics evolving toward consumption and bundle pricing - more flexibility for large enterprises, more cost complexity for smaller organizations.       | Organizations face growing complexity and cost without commensurate nonprofit-specific value in return. |
| B2C Scale Not the Core Assumption | Core CRM architecture remains built for commercial 82B workflows - not for the high-volume, B2C constituent model that nonprofits operate.                         | High constituent volume at nonprofit scale requires workarounds. Al-native capabilities require significant additional configuration for nonprofit workflows. |
| The Right Response                | Microsoft's trajectory is driven by enterprise and commercial software priorities - and delivers real value to organizations deeply embedded in that ecosystem. | Honest strategic assessment on our part and similar evaluation on your part. |
