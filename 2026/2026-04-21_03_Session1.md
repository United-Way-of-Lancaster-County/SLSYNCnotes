## 1: Why Data Quality Starts with People

Data gets entered the way it is easiest. Making it easier to enter good/correct data will result in better data

## 2: Why Bad Data Happens

### Four Patterns that Undermine Data Quality

- Friction
- Inconsistency
- No Feedback Loop
- **Workarounds**
	- Signs that the interface has not caught up with the workflow
	- Roadmap for what to address next

## 3: Designing Better Forms in Dynamics 365

Field ordering, required fields, and tooltips

- Put the most important fields first
- Evaluate which fields need to be required.
- Utilize tooltips!
	- From the field "Description"

Business Rules

Business rules let fields show, hide, or auto-populate based on context

*Every configuration later you add is a step the user no lonfer has to think about.*

Use filtered lookups to limit selection fields

## 5: Building a Culture of Data Consistency

Three Pillars of Data Consistency

- Living data standards
- Option sets over free text
- Data stewards

## 6: Listening to Your Users

- Watch for workarounds - They are your roadmap
- Schedule periodic form reviews - even once a year
- High impact, low effort changes go first - Quick wins build trust and keep feedback flowing

Three Things to Take Back to Your Team

- Make the right thing easy (and the wrong thing hard)
- Data quality is a team sport
- Small improvements compound
